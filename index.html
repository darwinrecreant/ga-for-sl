About

One of the difficulties professionals in Second Life have to deal with is the lack of feedback for their products. Currently, if you wanted to analyze how your product is used, what are the most liked/used features, or how engaging is your in-world store, you had to either make your own analytics storage and processing implementation, which is very costly, or rely on sub-par analytics services with limited features, or simply had to rely on anectdotal feedback from customers.

Google Analytics is the defacto standard analytics service for the web. It has an endless list of features, is very flexible, and did I mention free! Google Analytics for Second Life is a Second Life connector between Google Analytics and in-world objects. By opening a google analytics account and using this product it is possible to track analytics for your sales, products, parcels, vendors, games, and just about anything you can imagine that is scripted.

Who is this for

This product is geared towards sim/land owners and merchants. It provides an api for scripters to implement analytics in the product they are working on. There are even several general usecase sample scripts in the Script Examples tab above that will work by simply placing into your land or vendor as is.

What is possible with Google Analytics

Google Analytics can track things like:

  • Overall traffic to your sim or parcel with segmentable graphs
  • Duration of visits to to your parcel
  • Repeat visits and bounce rate
  • The most popular flows of visitors from parcel to parcel or room to room
  • Track revenue per store/vendor/product
  • Set conversion goals and alerts
  • Track promotions and their hit rates to your parcel

Privacy

Google Analytics for Second Life is a limited version of the web product, and therefore does not use cookies, has no access to visitor ip's, or any of the other features of the SL viewer. Out of the box, this product cannot track user data, other than simple counters for interactions within the sim. Storing user identifiying data, such as name or uuid, is up to the scripter's discretion, but is against Google Analytics' policies, as well as some European laws. The makers of Google Analytics for Second Life are not responsible for the misuse of Google and Second Life products.

GA for Sims / Parcels

Google Analytics for Sims allows sim owners to track visitors all across their sim, for the duration of their stay. Google Analytics for Parcels is identical to the sim product in every way, with the exception that the sim version can handle up to 280 events per minute (your mileage may vary based on sim performance), whereas the parcel version can handle up to 20 events per minute.

The package comes with the following contents:

  • GA Server (object) - The server object that handles all the tracking within the sim.
  • GA Relay (script) - The GA Server communicates with GA Relays that act as connectors between local scripts inside objects and the server.

The procedure to setup analytics tracking within the sim is as follows:

  1. Rez the GA Server somewhere discrete within the sim.
  2. Select the Create New option after clicking the server.
  3. Supply the UA code provided by google analytics, and give a label to this server. You can also decide if to set tracker access to Group or Owner (this applies to sending hit events; only owner has access to server menus). Setting to group will let your tenants or other team members use the relays to send hit events to the server, which will be useful if you are hosting an event, for example.
  4. Insert the GA Relay script in the scripted objects you would like to track, and link this relay to your GA Server by following the dialog popup instructions. A good place to start is with the greeter on your land, if you have one, to start tracking visits. There are some sample scripts you can use for this in the Script Examples tab above.
  5. If you or a colleague are up to the task, you can start tracking certain actions and events by using the open source scripts available in the Script Examples tab above, and script your own solutions for google analytics.

Important note: Once the server is rezzed and initialized, it would be a good idea to take a backup. If the server is deleted after you set up your relays, all relays will no longer work, and all the the relays will have to be set again one by one manually if you dont have a backup.

When rezzing a backup, all relays will be updated automatically, and the previous GA Server will reset.

When rezzing a fresh GA Server, such as during an upgrade with a newer version, you can choose the Replace option, to transfer the old server to this fresh one. All relays will link up automatically after you are done.
